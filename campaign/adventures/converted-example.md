# 🗝️ Converted Example — "The Toad-King's Cistern"

*Proof of concept for the [Conversion Playbook](../CONVERSION-PLAYBOOK.md): a found open module →
a playable campaign on our rules.*
**Source frame:** a representative **CC-BY-SA one-page dungeon** (a stand-in for any real
[One Page Dungeon Contest](https://www.dungeoncontest.com/) entry — substitute one with its
attribution). **Converted to:** 📚 Authentic 5e · Levels 2–3 · gothic-fey horror · Mature.

---

## ① THE RAW MODULE — the "found" input
*(terse, system-neutral, the way a one-page dungeon reads. CC-BY-SA, illustrative.)*

> **THE TOAD-KING'S CISTERN.** Under the drowned town of Mereholt squats the **Toad-King**, a bloated
> idol-thing that grants wishes for tongues.
> **Hook:** the well-water *sings* at night; children follow it down.
> **Rooms:** 1) the singing well-mouth · 2) the flooded stair (current; a drowned choir) · 3) the
> tongue-tithe shrine (cultists; a caged child) · 4) the spawn-pools (toad-swarms) · 5) the idol hall
> (the Toad-King).
> **Twist:** the wishes are *real* — and cost your voice.
> **Treasure:** the King's golden tongue.

---

## ② THE CONVERSION — engine output (pipeline applied)

**Step 1 · System/tone:** Authentic 5e · gothic-fey horror · **Guided** · Mature.

**Step 2 · Plot → Spine:**
- **b1 Inciting** — the singing well; a child (Wick) has already followed it down.
- **b2 Midpoint A** — the flooded stair + the drowned choir.
- **b3 Midpoint B** — the tongue-tithe shrine: the caged child + the *cost* revealed.
- **b4 Climax** — the Toad-King in the idol hall.
- **b5 Resolution** — the wish, the voice, the child, the water.

**Step 3 · Locations → Living-Map nodes:**
`n1` Well-Mouth (scene) → `n2` Flooded Stair (hazard) → `n3` Tithe Shrine (encounter) →
`n4` Spawn-Pools (encounter) → `n5` Idol Hall (boss). Edges run down the descent; a side-edge from n3
frees the child early (changes the shrine fight).

**Step 4 · Monsters → SRD stat blocks** *(verify exact lines vs SRD 5.2):*
- **Drowned Choir** (zombie) — AC 8, HP 22, Init −2, slam **+3, 1d6+1**; mindless (drops at 0).
- **Cultist** — AC 12, HP 9, Init +0, scimitar **+3, 1d6+1**.
- **Toad-Swarm** (swarm) — AC 12, HP 22, Init +1, bites **+3, 2d4** (half while bloodied); occupies a space.
- **The Toad-King** *(Large aberration)* — AC 14, HP 65, Init +2. **Multiattack:** tongue **+6** (reach
  15 ft, target grappled) + bite **+6, 2d8+4**. *Wish-Tithe* (recharge 5–6): offers a boon — **Cha
  save DC 14**; *accepting* grants the wish but **costs your voice** (no speech/verbal spells until the
  King dies). **Weak point:** its **golden tongue** (AC 16) — sever it (it has 15 HP) to end the
  Wish-Tithe and give the King disadvantage.

**Step 4.5 · DCs & guardrails (the fine-grain a live table needs):** n2 current — STR/Athletics
**DC 13** or swept 10 ft (a Drowned grapples the swept PC). Grapple & tongue **escape: STR/DEX vs
DC 14**. Shrine side-edge: **3 Cultists**; freeing Wick early = they fight at disadvantage and rout at
1 down. **Wish-Tithe scope:** the boon is *one significant favour the GM can honour* (a heal, a truth,
one creature raised, a door opened) — never an "I-win"; the lost voice returns when the King dies, or
via **Greater Restoration / Remove Curse**.

**Step 5 · Decisions → branches + endings:**
- Free the caged child early (alters the shrine fight) · **accept a wish** (power now, voice lost — a
  real moral fork) · **sever the tongue** (the clean win lever) vs grind the HP.
- **Endings:** *Mereholt's water runs clean* (King slain, tongue severed) · *A Wish Kept* (someone
  took the bargain — saved the day, lost their voice) · *The Golden Tongue Claimed* (a cursed relic — sequel hook).

**Step 6 · Wrap:** seed `state.json` with the party at `n1`; write the §12 save body; **attribution
line:** *"Adapted from <entry> by <author>, One Page Dungeon Contest, CC-BY-SA 4.0; SRD 5.2 per §17."*

## ③ Why this proves the engine
**Input** = a five-line, system-neutral dungeon. **Output** = a full **5-beat 5e campaign** with a
Living Map, SRD stat blocks, branches, a genuine moral fork (the voice-for-a-wish tithe), a weak-point
win lever, and a save — all generated by the **6-step pipeline**. *Any* open module converts the same
way; choose the System to taste, keep the attribution, and play.
