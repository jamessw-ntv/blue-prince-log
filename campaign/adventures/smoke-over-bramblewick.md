# 🔔 Smoke Over Bramblewick — Rules-Light One-Shot

*A complete, ready-to-run starter adventure for the AI Dungeon Master engine.*
**System:** 🎲 Rules-Light · **Levels:** 1 → 3 · **Length:** 1–2 sessions ·
**Tone:** Heroic folk-horror (PG-13) · **Content dials:** dread & body-horror kept
atmospheric, never graphic; honour `dial it back` / `fade to black` instantly.

> **GM:** This is a *module* in the engine's sense — the story layer only. Run it with
> the **Rules-Light** rules (Master file §7). Hold the rails loosely; the beats below are
> a spine, not a cage. Roll openly, fail forward, keep turns short.

---

## Premise
The hamlet of **Bramblewick** built its grist mill over a capped, flooded mineshaft. A
week ago the great mill-wheel stopped, the miller **Coll** vanished, and a sour **green
smoke** began seeping up from the old shaft. Livestock are found pale and bloodless. The
village reeve has scraped together a purse for anyone who'll go down and end it.

The truth: deep in the drowned gallery, the mine's cracked bronze **drowning-bell** has
become the heart of a spreading **spore-mass** — *The Choir* — that animates the dead and
exhales the smoke. Coll is alive, hiding, half-thralled. The bell can be **rung to stagger
the Choir**, or the hollow can be **burned** (kills it, scars the land) or **quelled** by
rite (harder, heals the land).

## Hook (inciting scene)
Dusk. The hero arrives as the **reeve, Old Hessa**, lights the last good lantern. A child,
**Pip**, tugs a sleeve: *"I saw lantern-eyes in the smoke. They blinked."* Hessa offers
**40 gold now, 40 on return**, and the run of the mill. The smoke thickens as you speak.

## Cast
- **Old Hessa** — reeve; tired, honest, frightened. Knows the mill was built over the mine.
- **Pip** — child; saw the "lantern-eyes" (the Choir's thralls); brave, unreliable.
- **Bram the Peddler** — a stranger camped at the well; **lies** (Insight DC 13). He's a
  relic-scavenger who *uncapped the shaft* looking for the bronze bell and ran. Comic-shifty,
  not evil; can be turned into a guide.
- **Miller Coll** — alive in the mine (Act 2), spore-sickened, terrified. Carries the
  **Miller's Hammer**. Treating him: Medicine/INT **DC 13**.

- **Lysa** — Bard-healer. *Warm, sardonic, curious.* Drive: a song worth singing. Signature:

## Act 1 — The Village (investigate)
Three leads; two are enough to move on. Award a level (→2) after Act 1 if the table is new.
- **The Mill.** Investigation **DC 12** finds a flour-dusted **trapdoor** over the shaft and
  drag-marks. Perception **DC 13**: the marks lead *down*, not away — Coll was taken, then
  *walked back* on his own.
- **The Well.** Bram the Peddler. Insight **DC 13** catches the lie; Persuasion/Intimidation
  **DC 13** turns him into a reluctant guide (he knows the **bell** is down there and that
  **fire** "made the green stuff flinch").
- **Pip's story.** Persuasion **DC 12** (or just kindness): the thralls have **lantern-eyes**
  and *"hum the same note."* She presses a **lucky tin whistle** on the hero (flavour: grants
  one auto-success on a *Frightened* save — single use).

**Branch:** trust Hessa's "burn it" instinct, or Bram's "the bell's worth a fortune" angle,
or Pip's "the note can be un-hummed." These bias the Act 3 ending the table reaches for.

## Act 2 — The Flooded Mine (explore) — *map renders on the dashboard*
Capped shaft → ladder down into cold green haze. Keep the map small; move `@` as they go.

1. **Entry Shaft.** Cramped; a thrall (see stats) shambles up the ladder — a sharp first
   fight to set tone. Fungal light, dripping.
2. **The Drowned Gallery (hazard).** Black water rising. **STR/Athletics DC 13** to cross a
   collapsed span or be swept 10 ft and drop a non-stowed item (1d4 bludgeoning). Stealth here
   avoids waking the warren.
3. **The Spore Warren.** The drained livestock; **3 Spore-Thralls** tending a pulsing pod.
   Investigation **DC 12**: the pod "answers" a low bell-note from deeper in.
4. **Coll's Refuge.** A timbered nook; **Miller Coll**, spore-veined, clutching the
   **Miller's Hammer**. Medicine/INT **DC 13** clears his head enough to talk: *"The bell.
   It rings in my teeth. Strike it and it forgets you — but only once."* (Sets up Act 3.)
5. **The Bell Chamber.** A cracked bronze bell half-sunk in spore-flesh. The way to the Heart.

### Encounter stats (Rules-Light)
- **Spore-Thrall** — HP **9**, AC **12**, Speed 20, **Init +0**. *Spore claw* **+3**, **1d6+1**; on a hit,
  target makes **CON DC 12** or **Poisoned** until end of next turn. Drops to ash at 0 HP.
- **Drowning hazard** — recurring; **DC 13** STR or DEX (player's framing), fail = swept + 1d4.

## Act 3 — The Hollow Heart (climax + choice)
The bell tolls without a hand. Behind it, **The Choir** unfolds: a spore-mass wearing the
bell like a crown, voiced by the campaign's chosen register (mournful, not cruel).

### Boss — The Choir
- HP **38**, AC **13**, Speed 10 (rooted; reaches the whole chamber), **Init +0**.
- **Spore Lash** **+5**, **1d8+2** (two per round).
- **Resonant Toll** *(recharge 5–6)*: everyone within 20 ft — **CON DC 13** or **Frightened**
  (save ends) **+2d6 psychic**. Pip's whistle / *Bless* / *Bardic Inspiration* shine here.
- **Stagger:** strike the cracked bell with the **Miller's Hammer** (an Action, no roll) →
  the Choir has **disadvantage for 1 round** and skips its next Toll. Usable **once** (as Coll
  warned). Clever players who set this up should feel rewarded.

### The choice (how it ends)
- **Burn the hollow** (Hessa's way): destroy it outright — fire/oil + a finishing blow.
  The Choir dies screaming; the mine is **scarred**, the smoke gone. Clean, a little grim.
- **The Quelling Rite** (Pip/Lysa's way): *un-hum the note.* During the fight, **3 successful**
  WIS/CHA/Performance checks (**DC 14**) "answer" the bell's tone. On the 3rd, the spores still
  and **fall to harmless ash**; Coll and the land recover. Harder, but the **good ending**.
- **Mercy / other:** reward improvisation — freeing Coll first, flooding the chamber, etc.

## Rewards
- **XP / level:** finish at **Level 2–3**.
- **Loot:**
  - **Miller's Hammer** — *Uncommon.* +1 to hit/damage; once/day **Toll**: stagger one foe
    (disadvantage 1 round).
  - **Spore-Ash Cloak** — *Uncommon.* Advantage on saves vs. **Poisoned**.
  - **~40–80 gold** + Hessa's second purse.
- **Title:** **"Bramblewick's Lantern"** (companions' approval +1; villagers shelter you).

## Epilogue beats (pick to match the ending)
- **Quelled:** the wheel turns again; Coll names a loaf after the hero; green meadow over the
  capped shaft. Pip keeps the whistle "for next time."
- **Burned:** the smoke is gone but nothing grows on the scar; Hessa is grateful and a little
  haunted. Bram tries to buy the slagged bell.
- **Failed/fled:** the smoke spreads to the next valley — a thread for a sequel.
